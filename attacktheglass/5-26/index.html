These rankings assign each player a value as an overall dynasty asset - determined at an intersection of current impact, projected future impact, and trade value. My player evaluation assumes that you are in a league that will last atleast longer than 3 years into the future, and incorporates standard 9-category format factors like stat scarcity and punt value.

# Name Age Thoughts
1 LUKA DONCIC 23.2 For all of Luka's all-time great qualities, he honestly has still stagnated as it comes to fantasy. He remains one of the most negative FG/FT% impact players in the league (considering his volume) to go with very mediocre defensive stats. There really is a case to be made that Lamelo is a better pick here with what I consider to be a slightly more desirable stat-profile but I have to admit that there is something to how transcendent Luka has become from a real-NBA perspective. It really makes you think he is one of these all-time great players who will be able to improve these things in coming seasons as he works into his prime.
2 LAMELO BALL 20.8
One of the most impressive showings as a sophomore player I've ever seen. As fantasy stats goes, he is basically doing everything Luka is but with elite FT% and STL instead of a few extra points - but I think more of that will come. A player being able to put up top-15 value in his 2nd season is crazy, and it seems we're likely about to witness a machine-like 24/8/8/2 for the next 10+ years and one of the best fantasy players ever. I'd be completely happy taking him #1 if you'd prefer his stat-profile.
3 NIKOLA JOKIC 27.3 He was the best fantasy player last year by a mile, and is the single most winning player in the league with his absolutely insane out-of-position value in AST/FT/STL. He also has the potential to have a much longer prime than most elite centers because of his slow cerebral game.
4 TRAE YOUNG 23.7 A disappointing Hawks seasons doesn't give me any disillusions about Trae's fantasy dominance. He's clearly the next coming of Damian Lillard/James Harden but got himself to that level at a much earlier age. Elite assists, points, and FT% impact will always be one of the most highly coveted stat combinations and he's the best young player by far in that regard.
5 JAYSON TATUM 24.2 Yet another top-15 season for Tatum and he's only 24 years old. He seems to have fully arrived as an MVP caliber player moving forward, and Boston has repeatedly refused to pull the trigger on big free agent deals, so nothing really stands in the way of Tatum being whatever he wants to be.
6 GIANNIS ANTETOKOUNMPO 27.5 Giannis put up his best fantasy season ever this year and finally cracked the top-10 to shut up all the constant gossip about his poor shooting. He is who he is, and now it's just about how long he can do it. Your guess is as good as mine on that, but he needs to be picked up here regardless because he is the most dominant player in the league in punt strategies.
7 KARL-ANTHONY TOWNS 26.5 Coming up on his age 27 season, the youth value is slowly slipping and Edwards is dragging on his ceiling. Still, getting huge 3PM and FT% impact from a big man is just too incredible to pass up further than this. He brings absurd value across 9-cats and puts up top-5 seasons like it's nothing. Should be able to easily have a longer career than most centers with his elite shooting touch.
8 CADE CUNNINGHAM 20.7 He didn't make quite the huge splash we may have hoped for at his ADP but it became very clear as the season went on - Cade is the real deal and he's going to be good. His fantasy profile is everything we covet in a young stud player, and I expect year 2 to be a huge explosion for him, so get your hands on him if you still can.
9 DEJOUNTE MURRAY 25.7 A monster has been unleashed in San Antonio. He may not have the name brand value of a Lamelo/Luka, but if you really look at the numbers - its plain and simple - a 25 year old whose posting 20/9/9 with 2+ stls cannot be left off the first round of a dynasty draft under any circumstances. If you can get him for a bargain before people realize this - do it.
10 ANTHONY EDWARDS 20.8 Edwards is clearly an offensive monster in the making and despite the pre-draft chatter about his lethargic defense, he's consistently put up elite steals numbers since his rookie season breakout. Efficiency and assists have improved this year and he's already one of the most dominant scorers in the league. The sky is the limit in my eyes.
